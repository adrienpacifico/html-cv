During my PhD, I worked on the empirical economics of taxation. My thesis is composed of three chapters: The first one focuses on the impact of tax frequency and shows that increasing the pace at which a citizen is taxed can significantly improve their welfare. The second one focuses on the optimal allocation of children on tax returns, and it shows that couples who optimize tend to marry more the following year (while those who don't tend to divorce more). The third one studied the empirical implications of diminished tax breaks related to children (triple-difference estimation).
